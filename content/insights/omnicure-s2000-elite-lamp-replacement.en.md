The OmniCure S2000 Elite is a high-performance UV spot curing system designed for precise adhesive curing, bonding, and production applications. To maintain stable UV output and repeatable curing performance, the lamp module must be installed correctly and replaced when required.

This guide explains how to install or replace the **OmniCure S2000 Elite lamp module**, including the **012-68000R Standard Curing Lamp Module** and the **012-69000R Surface Cure Lamp Module**.

## Before You Start

Before opening the unit or handling the lamp module, always follow proper safety procedures.

Make sure the AC power cord is disconnected from the system. If the unit has been running, allow the lamp module to cool down completely before opening the lamp housing. UV lamps operate at high temperature, and improper handling may shorten lamp life or create safety risks.

When handling the lamp module, avoid touching the bulb glass envelope or the inside surface of the reflector. Skin oils can cause premature lamp failure. Hold the lamp only by the ceramic component and glass rim.

## Choose the Correct OmniCure S2000 Elite Lamp Module

The OmniCure S2000 Elite system supports different lamp options depending on the curing application.

For standard adhesive curing, use the appropriate standard lamp module. For applications requiring stronger surface curing performance, the surface cure lamp module may be selected.

If you are not sure which lamp type is suitable for your adhesive, substrate, or curing process, contact ETIA technical support before replacement.

## Step-by-Step OmniCure S2000 Elite Lamp Module Installation

### Step 1: Disconnect Power

Turn off the OmniCure S2000 Elite system and disconnect the AC power cord from the unit.

Do not open the lamp housing while the system is connected to power.

### Step 2: Allow the Lamp to Cool

If the system has been operating, allow the lamp module to cool completely before opening the unit.

This is important for operator safety and to prevent damage during handling.

### Step 3: Remove the Lamp Access Side Panel

Use the provided 3mm Allen key to loosen and remove the screws from the lamp access side panel.

Carefully remove the side panel from the unit cover and place it in a safe location.

### Step 4: Remove the Lamp Module from Packaging

Carefully remove the lamp module from its container.

Hold the lamp only by the ceramic component and glass rim. Do not touch the bulb glass envelope, reflector surface, circuit board, or sensitive connector areas.

### Step 5: Position the Lamp Module

Position the lamp so that it faces toward the front of the unit, with the power leads facing toward you.

Make sure the middle of the lamp is aligned with the spring clamp. Slide the lamp into position until it snaps securely into the spring clamp.

The leading edge of the reflector should fit snugly into the lamp holder recess.

### Step 6: Connect the Intelli-Lamp® Sensor Connector

Locate the 6-pin Intelli-Lamp® sensor connector at the rear of the lamp module.

Connect it to the matching connector behind the lamp housing wall. The Intelli-Lamp connector only fits in the correct orientation. If it does not connect easily, rotate it 180 degrees and try again.

After connection, tuck the Intelli-Lamp wires into the grommet located on the divider wall between the lamp and connector area.

This step is important. If the wires are not positioned correctly, the door may not close completely and the system may not power on.

### Step 7: Connect the Lamp Power Connector

Locate the lamp power connector with two leads and connect it to its matching connector on the bottom of the lamp housing wall.

The lamp power connector also fits only in the correct orientation. If it does not insert easily, rotate it 180 degrees and reconnect.

### Step 8: Check Lamp Alignment

Before closing the unit, confirm that the lamp anode cooling fin and lamp power lead at the front of the lamp are centered within the lamp holder cutout.

Rotate the lamp slightly if required.

Correct lamp alignment helps ensure proper installation and stable operation.

### Step 9: Reinstall the Lamp Housing Side Panel

Replace the lamp housing side panel and tighten the fastening screws securely.

If the lamp housing panel is not fully secured, the system may not power on.

### Step 10: Reconnect Power and Start the System

Reconnect the AC power cord and turn on the power switch at the back of the unit.

Once the system initializes, allow the lamp to warm up properly before starting production exposure. A stable warm-up period helps ensure consistent optical output.

## Important Handling Notes

- Do not touch the bulb glass envelope.
- Do not touch the inside surface of the reflector.
- Do not force any connector into place.
- Do not operate the system with the lamp housing panel open.
- Do not look directly into UV light output.
- Always use appropriate UV protective eyewear and skin protection.

## OmniCure S2000 Elite Lamp Replacement — Common Questions

### Which lamp module should I use for OmniCure S2000 Elite?

For OmniCure S2000 Elite, the main lamp module options are **012-68000R S2000 Elite Lamp Module – Standard** and **012-69000R S2000 Elite Lamp Module – Surface Cure**.

### Is 012-64000R the same as 012-68000R?

No. **012-64000R** is commonly used as a standard spare 200W lamp for S2000 and S1500 systems, while **012-68000R** refers to the S2000 Elite Lamp Module – Standard. Always confirm the exact system model and part number before ordering.

### Why does the system not power on after lamp replacement?

Check whether the lamp housing side panel is fully secured. Also confirm that the Intelli-Lamp wires are properly tucked into the grommet and that all connectors are fully seated.

### Why should I use a genuine OmniCure lamp?

A genuine OmniCure lamp helps maintain correct optical output, lamp tracking, system detection, and curing process stability. Non-genuine or incorrectly installed lamps may affect curing performance, process repeatability, and equipment reliability.
