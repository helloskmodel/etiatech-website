The OmniCure S2000 Elite is a high-performance lamp-based UV spot curing system designed for precision adhesive curing, bonding, and manufacturing applications. Because the S2000 Elite uses a broad-spectrum mercury UV lamp, the optical bandpass filter plays an important role in controlling which wavelength range reaches the curing site.

Choosing the correct filter can affect cure depth, surface cure, heat exposure, material compatibility, and process repeatability.

This guide explains the common **OmniCure S2000 Elite filter options**, including **365nm**, **320–390nm**, **400–500nm**, **250–450nm**, and **320–500nm**, and how to select the right one for your UV curing process.

## Why Filters Matter in UV Curing

The OmniCure S2000 Elite lamp produces broad-spectrum UV output. A bandpass filter limits that output to a defined wavelength range.

This helps manufacturers control:

- UV wavelength delivered to the adhesive
- Cure depth and surface cure behavior
- Heat exposure on sensitive substrates
- Compatibility with adhesive photoinitiators
- Process repeatability across production batches
- Risk of overexposure or incomplete cure

In simple terms, the lamp generates the energy, while the filter shapes the wavelength window used for curing.

## OmniCure S2000 Elite 365nm Filter

The **365nm filter** is one of the most commonly requested UV curing filter options.

It is often used when the adhesive or coating is designed to respond strongly to 365nm UVA energy. Many UV-curable adhesives are formulated around 365nm or nearby UVA wavelengths, making this filter a practical choice for general bonding and process development.

### Best for:

- UV adhesives designed for 365nm curing
- General precision bonding
- Electronics assembly
- Optical bonding
- Medical device assembly
- Applications needing controlled UVA exposure

### Why choose 365nm?

Choose the 365nm filter when your adhesive datasheet recommends 365nm curing or when you need a controlled UVA wavelength instead of a broader output window.

## OmniCure S2000 Elite 320–390nm Filter

The **320–390nm filter** provides a wider UVA curing window than a narrow 365nm filter. This can be useful when the adhesive responds across a broader UVA range or when the process benefits from more total UV energy within the UVA band.

Compared with a narrow 365nm filter, the 320–390nm option can support faster curing for some materials because it allows a wider portion of the UVA spectrum to reach the curing site.

### Best for:

- UVA-sensitive adhesives
- Fast adhesive curing
- Production bonding
- Plastic and glass bonding
- Applications requiring broader UVA output
- Process windows where 365nm alone is too narrow

### Why choose 320–390nm?

Choose 320–390nm when your adhesive can absorb across a wider UVA range and you want a balance of curing speed, depth, and process control.

## OmniCure S2000 Elite 400–500nm Filter

The **400–500nm filter** allows longer wavelength energy in the violet to visible range.

This filter may be useful when curing materials that respond to longer wavelengths or when the substrate is sensitive to shorter UV wavelengths. Longer wavelengths can sometimes penetrate more deeply into certain materials and may reduce the effect of shorter-wavelength UV exposure on sensitive parts.

### Best for:

- Visible-light-sensitive adhesives
- Deeper curing in selected materials
- UV-sensitive substrates
- Applications where shorter UV wavelengths should be reduced
- Specialty adhesive processes

### Why choose 400–500nm?

Choose 400–500nm when the adhesive is designed for longer wavelength curing or when the process needs to reduce exposure to shorter UV wavelengths.

## OmniCure S2000 Elite 250–450nm Filter

The **250–450nm filter** provides a broad UV output window. It includes shorter UV wavelengths as well as UVA and near-visible wavelengths.

This option can be useful for applications requiring strong surface cure or where the material benefits from a wider spectral distribution. However, because shorter UV wavelengths can be more aggressive, this filter should be selected carefully based on adhesive chemistry, substrate sensitivity, and operator safety controls.

### Best for:

- Broad-spectrum UV curing
- Surface cure applications
- Acrylic resin applications
- Process development
- Applications where shorter UV energy is beneficial

### Why choose 250–450nm?

Choose 250–450nm when the process requires a wider UV spectrum and the material can tolerate shorter UV wavelengths.

## OmniCure S2000 Elite 320–500nm Filter

The **320–500nm filter** provides a broad UVA to visible wavelength range. This can be a flexible option for production lines using different adhesives or for laboratories testing multiple materials.

It allows more wavelength coverage than 320–390nm and includes longer wavelengths up to 500nm.

### Best for:

- Flexible production environments
- R&D and adhesive testing
- Multi-adhesive process development
- Applications needing UVA plus visible-range energy
- General-purpose broad-window curing

### Why choose 320–500nm?

Choose 320–500nm when you need a broader curing window and want one filter to support multiple adhesive types or process trials.

## How to Choose the Right OmniCure S2000 Elite Filter

The best OmniCure S2000 Elite filter depends on the adhesive, substrate, cure depth requirement, and process target.

Use the adhesive manufacturer's recommended wavelength as the first reference. If the adhesive datasheet recommends 365nm, start with the 365nm filter. If the adhesive accepts a broader UVA range, 320–390nm or 320–500nm may improve process flexibility. If the adhesive is designed for longer wavelength curing, consider 400–500nm.

For surface cure issues, such as tacky surfaces or oxygen inhibition, a broader or shorter-wavelength option may be worth testing. For heat-sensitive or UV-sensitive substrates, a narrower or longer-wavelength filter may reduce unnecessary exposure.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the most common OmniCure S2000 Elite filter?

The 365nm filter is commonly used because many UV adhesives are formulated for UVA curing around 365nm. However, the best filter depends on the adhesive and substrate.

### What is the difference between 365nm and 320–390nm?

The 365nm filter provides a more focused wavelength range around 365nm. The 320–390nm filter allows a broader UVA range, which may improve curing speed or flexibility for adhesives that absorb across multiple UVA wavelengths.

### When should I use the 400–500nm filter?

Use the 400–500nm filter when your adhesive is designed for longer wavelength or visible-light curing, or when you need to reduce shorter UV exposure on sensitive substrates.

### Can I change the filter myself?

The OmniCure S2000 Elite is designed for user reconfiguration with different optical bandpass filters. However, proper handling and safety procedures should always be followed. If you are unsure, contact ETIA technical support.

### Do I need to revalidate the process after changing filters?

Yes. Any filter change can affect cure speed, cure depth, surface finish, and final bond strength. Always revalidate the curing process after changing the filter, lamp, light guide, adhesive, or curing distance.
