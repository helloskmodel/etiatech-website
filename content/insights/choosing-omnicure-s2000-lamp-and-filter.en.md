The OmniCure S2000 Elite is one of the most flexible lamp-based UV spot curing systems on the market — precisely because it is *reconfigurable*. You can change the lamp module and the optical band-pass filter in the field, and the system auto-detects the new configuration. That flexibility is powerful, but it also raises a practical question every process engineer eventually asks: **which lamp and which filter do I actually need?**

This guide walks through the decision the way we do it with customers — starting from the adhesive, not the hardware.

## Step 1 — Start with your adhesive's photoinitiator

Every UV-curable adhesive cures fastest at the wavelength its photoinitiator absorbs most strongly. The manufacturer's technical data sheet will list an absorption peak (or a recommended cure wavelength) — that number is the single most important input to your lamp and filter choice.

- Photoinitiators that absorb in the **long-wave UVA / visible** band (≈365–420 nm) cover the majority of one-part UV adhesives used in electronics and medical bonding.
- Some chemistries need **short-wave UV** energy to overcome oxygen inhibition at the surface.

Once you know where your adhesive absorbs, the hardware choice becomes straightforward.

## Step 2 — Choose the lamp module: Standard or Surface Cure

The S2000 Elite accepts two field-replaceable, plug-and-play Intelli-Lamp® modules. Both use a genuine 200 W mercury short-arc lamp; they differ in how the output spectrum is shaped.

| Module | Part number | Spare 200 W lamp | Best for |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Standard** | 012-68000R | 012-64000R | The widest range of UV adhesive bonding — the default for most applications |
| **Surface Cure** | 012-69000R | 012-54000R | Tack-free, seamless finishes on acrylic resins where oxygen inhibition leaves a sticky surface |

If your parts come out of the cure fully bonded but with a slightly tacky top surface, that is the classic sign of **oxygen inhibition** — and the Surface Cure module is designed to solve exactly that by reshaping the power spectrum toward the short-wave UV that cures the surface layer.

> Note: `012-64000R` (a standard spare 200 W lamp for the S2000 and S1500) is **not** the same as `012-68000R` (the S2000 Elite Standard Lamp Module). Always confirm your system model and part number before ordering.

## Step 3 — Select the optical band-pass filter

On top of the lamp, the S2000 Elite lets you insert a selectable optical band-pass filter to narrow the output to the band your chemistry wants — cutting unwanted heat and unnecessary wavelengths.

| Filter part number | Pass band |
| --- | --- |
| 019-00387R | 400–500 nm |
| 019-00388R | 365 nm |
| 019-00389R | 320–390 nm |
| 019-00390R | 250–450 nm |
| 019-00391R | 320–500 nm |
| 019-00392R | Blank (full spectrum, no band-pass) |

A few rules of thumb:

1. **Match the band to the absorption peak.** If your adhesive peaks at 365 nm, the 365 nm or 320–390 nm filter concentrates energy where it counts.
2. **Reduce heat on sensitive parts** by cutting the visible/IR tail with a narrower band such as 365 nm.
3. **Maximize raw throughput** on heat-tolerant parts with the broad 320–500 nm filter or the blank position.

The S2000 Elite auto-detects whichever filter you install and adjusts its operating parameters — but you should always **recalibrate with the OmniCure R2000 radiometer** after any lamp or filter change before returning to production.

## Step 4 — Lock in repeatability

Selecting the right lamp and filter gets you the right *spectrum*. Getting the right *dose*, cycle after cycle, is what Closed-Loop Feedback (±5%) and R2000 calibration deliver. For validated medical and automotive processes, that repeatability is not optional — it is the reason to run an S2000 Elite in the first place.
